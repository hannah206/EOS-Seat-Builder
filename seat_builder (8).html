Key responsibilities 3–7 outcomes ▶

List outcomes this seat owns — not daily tasks. Each should be something you can hold this seat accountable for in an L10.

More than 7? Either split the seat or move the excess to a process doc.

Part two — Person in seat
With the seat clearly defined above, now evaluate the person currently filling it. The seat sets the standard — the person is measured against it, not the other way around.

Person in seat Who holds this seat today ▶
F
GWC assessment Get it · Want it · Capacity ▶

All three must be YES for the right person to be in the right seat. Answer honestly — this is a leadership conversation, not a performance review.

G
Get it

Do they truly understand this seat? Do they intuitively grasp what success looks like and how it connects to the rest of the team? This isn't about intelligence — it's about alignment with the function.

W
Want it

Do they genuinely want this seat? Are they motivated by the role itself — not just the title, the pay, or the comfort of familiarity? Would they choose this seat again?

C
Capacity

Do they have the time, skills, and bandwidth to execute this seat at the level the business needs? Capacity gaps that aren't named become accountability gaps.
